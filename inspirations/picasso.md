# Picasso — distort until it is more true

Picasso's bull: eleven lithographs, each one removing lines, until four
strokes ARE the bull. Iterate like that.

- Abstraction by subtraction: take the current champion and remove —
  narrow the waist harder, thin the plates, shrink the deck — until it
  breaks, then add back exactly one stroke. The last valid design
  before failure is the four-stroke bull.
- Cubism shows one object from several viewpoints at once. A frame in
  a crosswind IS seen from several viewpoints at once: nose-on by the
  headwind, side-on by the gust. Design a frame whose front view and
  side view belong to two different animals — slender from the front,
  nearly absent from the side.
- Distortion of proportion is his signature: the enormous hand, the
  tiny head. Exaggerate one gene far beyond good taste — grotesquely
  long arms on a tiny plate, or a huge plate with stubby arms — and let
  the rest of the body reorganize around the exaggeration.
- Blue period / rose period: commit to a palette. Do an all-stiffness
  period (carbon, thick, wide) and an all-lightness period (PA12-CF,
  thin, waisted) as separate proposals rather than always mixing a
  compromise.
- "Good artists copy, great artists steal." Steal the best single gene
  from each of three different elites and force them into one body,
  even if they never evolved together.
