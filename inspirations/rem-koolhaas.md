# Rem Koolhaas — program over form

Design the way OMA designs: start from the program, not the silhouette,
and let the collisions show.

- The frame's "program" is the stack: FC, ESC, battery, wiring. Stop
  hiding it. Make the deck gap generous and unapologetic — a void is a
  program element, not wasted space. What does the frame look like when
  the electronics bay is the protagonist and the arms are merely
  infrastructure serving it?
- "F*** context." The stock TBS geometry is context. Refuse to be a
  polite perturbation of it: if the sweep wants to be extreme, grow the
  plates to make it legal instead of retreating toward stock.
- Bigness: past a certain scale, a building becomes its own city. Try
  the largest coherent frame — long arms, stretched plates — and see if
  disk loading economics beat aerodynamic sleekness.
- Junkspace: celebrate the cheap and generic. PETG and PLA+, the
  air-conditioned shopping-mall materials of 3D printing — can a
  brilliant plan win with a mediocre material, the way a generic tower
  wins with a brilliant section?
- Cross-programming: the battery is also ballast, also a spine, also a
  fairing. Tilt it until it stops being a box the frame carries and
  becomes a wall the frame is organized against.
